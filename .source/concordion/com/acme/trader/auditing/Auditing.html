Auditing and History Overview

Auditing

The system will attempt to record all changes to an entity in the form of auditing data. The state of the entity along with information around the date the entity was changed and who changed it will be stored.

For example, a full audit, reflecting every change to some entity (in this case a house price) might look something like the following

Price Record time Updated in / Active from
£250,000 2007 First Quarter of 2007
£245,000 2007 Third Quarter of 2007
£230,000 2009 2009
£225,000 2010 2010

History

In contrast to full auditing, history reflects the most relevant version of an entity for a given point in time (called the record time).

Continuing the example above, when asking for history for a specific record time the following would hold

Requested Record Time Price Active from Active to
First Quarter of 2007 £250,000 First Quarter of 2007 Second Quarter of 2007
2008 £245,000 Third Quarter of 2007 2009
2011 £225,000 2011 (until updated)

Terms

  • Record Time is the point in time at which the entity is relevant, for example, the entity may be active for a specific date.
  • Real or Actual Time is the point in time at which the entity audit was created. For example, lets say an entity was created on Monday with a record time reflecting that the entity was active on that Monday. Now, lets say the entity was updated on Tuesday (still with a record time on the Monday). The record date stays the same but the real time (the time the data was written) for the most recent record time has changed to Tuesday.
  • A record is Active for a given point in time if the record has a record time less than or equal to the query time and if multiple records exists, the most recent in terms of the time it was updated (the active from column in the above example).
